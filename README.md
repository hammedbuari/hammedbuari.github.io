About

Hello, I’m Hammed Babatunde Buari.

I specialize in evaluating AI-generated work through structured analysis, evidence-based reasoning, and meticulous quality review. My work sits at the intersection of human judgment and machine intelligence—where accuracy, consistency, and thoughtful evaluation matter most.

My professional background combines analytical thinking, software development training, user experience evaluation, and practical experience reviewing complex outputs against defined standards. I enjoy breaking problems into observable evidence, identifying inconsistencies, and communicating actionable feedback that improves overall quality.

This repository represents my public digital home.

Rather than presenting isolated projects, it demonstrates how I think, how I evaluate, and how I approach quality assurance for modern AI systems.

⸻

What I Do

* Evaluate AI-generated responses using structured quality frameworks
* Review reasoning, factual accuracy, and logical consistency
* Assess user experience and interaction quality
* Identify hallucinations, ambiguity, and instruction-following failures
* Produce evidence-based written feedback
* Analyze prompts and response behavior
* Perform comparative response evaluation
* Support model alignment through systematic human judgment

Core Principles

Everything in this repository is built around a small set of engineering principles.

Evidence First

Claims should always be supported by observable evidence.

Quality Before Speed

Fast work has little value without accuracy.

Consistency Matters

Evaluation should remain reliable regardless of task complexity.

Human Judgment

AI performs best when guided by disciplined human reviewers.

Continuous Improvement

Every evaluation should contribute toward building better AI systems.

Evaluation Workflow

Every review follows a repeatable process.

Prompt
      │
      ▼
Model Response
      │
      ▼
Evidence Collection
      │
      ▼
Quality Assessment
      │
      ▼
Written Evaluation
      │
      ▼
Recommendations

The objective is not simply to score outputs.

The objective is to understand why an output succeeds or fails and communicate that reasoning clearly.
